Before we start

Two things you need to enable on your Synology NAS before using this tool.

Enable SSH on your Synology NAS
1
Open DSM → Control Panel → Terminal & SNMP
2
Tick Enable SSH service. Port defaults to 22 — leave it unless you changed it.
3
Click Apply.
SSH is needed for SFTP file transfer. You can disable it again after the transfer if you prefer.
Find your NAS destination path
1
In DSM open File Station and navigate to the photo shared folder.
2
The real disk path is /volume1/photo — not /photo. Most Synology NAS units use volume1. Use this as your destination base.
If you have a DS923+ or a multi-volume NAS, it may be /volume2/photo etc. Check in Storage Manager → Volume.
⚠ Common "Access Denied" cause: Using /photo/... instead of /volume1/photo/... is the #1 reason SFTP uploads fail on Synology. Always use the full volume path.

What to do next
1
Open Synology Photos on your NAS — your photos will appear indexed automatically.
2
Install Synology Photos on your phone and enable auto-backup for future photos.
3
Once confirmed, delete the Takeout zip files from your PC to free up space.
4
You can now disable SSH on the NAS again: DSM → Control Panel → Terminal & SNMP → uncheck Enable SSH.
